RISK WEIGHTING AND MATERIALITY RULES:

Fraud signals must be evaluated using:
- recency
- correlation strength
- behavioral abnormality
- transaction materiality
- reinforcing evidence
- customer context

Do NOT treat all signals equally.

--------------------------------------------------

SIGNAL STRENGTH PRINCIPLES:

1. Weak standalone signals should remain LOW severity.

Examples:
- old profile changes
- low-dollar payments
- known device usage
- same IP/location
- isolated rule triggers
- single failed login
- established payee usage

These do NOT independently indicate fraud.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Multiple weak signals may become MEDIUM/HIGH only when strongly correlated.

Examples:
- profile update + new device + rapid payment
- failed login + password reset + payee add
- geo mismatch + shared device + instant payment

--------------------------------------------------

3. Transaction amount/materiality affects severity.

Small-dollar transactions:
- may indicate testing/probing behavior
- should usually remain LOW or MEDIUM severity
- unless reinforced by multiple strong anomalies

High-dollar or unusual transactions:
- increase severity significantly
- especially with profile/device/payee anomalies

Examples:
- $10 after profile change -> LOW/MEDIUM
- $5000 instant payment after profile change from new device -> HIGH

--------------------------------------------------

4. Known/trusted behavior reduces severity.

Reduce severity when:
- device is historically known
- IP/location matches customer profile
- payee is established
- no prior fraud exists
- no authentication anomalies exist
- customer behavior matches historical patterns

--------------------------------------------------

5. Recency matters.

Recent suspicious behavior:
- increases severity

Old historical behavior:
- should not increase severity alone

--------------------------------------------------

6. Sequence compression increases severity.

Examples:
- login -> payee add -> transfer within minutes
- multiple TWIs in seconds/minutes
- rapid retries/rejections

--------------------------------------------------

7. Real-time or irreversible payment methods increase severity.

Examples:
- instant payment
- real-time transfer
- prepaid movement
- card-to-card transfer

Especially when combined with:
- new payee
- profile changes
- risky device
- geo mismatch

--------------------------------------------------

8. Prior confirmed fraud increases severity only when behavior is behaviorally related.

Do NOT automatically elevate severity solely because prior fraud exists.

--------------------------------------------------

9. Generate HIGH severity only when:
- multiple strong indicators correlate
OR
- severe behavioral anomaly exists

--------------------------------------------------

10. Avoid exaggerated fraud narratives.

Do NOT:
- overstate weak signals
- assume account takeover without sufficient evidence
- generate HIGH severity from isolated anomalies
- describe normal behavior as suspicious

--------------------------------------------------

SEVERITY GUIDELINES:

HIGH:
- strong multi-signal correlation
- rapid suspicious sequence
- high materiality
- risky device/IP behavior
- account takeover patterns
- mule behavior indicators
- real-time movement with reinforcing anomalies

MEDIUM:
- moderate anomaly
- partial suspicious sequence
- moderate materiality
- suspicious but incomplete evidence

LOW:
- weak anomaly
- informational pattern
- low materiality
- limited supporting evidence
- insufficient correlation
